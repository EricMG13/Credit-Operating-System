# .impeccable

Persistent design context for **CAOS — Credit Agent OS**. This file is the
source of truth for design decisions across all sessions. The visual
vocabulary below is already established in code ([globals.css](caos/frontend/src/app/globals.css),
[tailwind.config.js](caos/frontend/tailwind.config.js)); the principles
govern how to extend it.

## Design Context

### Users

The product is an **institutional leveraged-finance credit analysis platform**
(high-yield / loan universe), surfaced as five analyst "concepts": Command
Center (A), Pipeline (B), Deep-Dive (C), Model Builder (D), Report Studio (E).
Three personas use it, but **when trade-offs force a choice, optimize for the
buy-side credit analyst** — the person doing the deep work.

- **Buy-side credit analyst (primary).** Lives in Deep-Dive, Model Builder, and
  Report Studio. Context: building a defensible credit view under time
  pressure, moving between source documents, the model, and the narrative.
  Job-to-be-done: reach a conclusion they can stand behind in front of an
  investment committee, with every claim traceable to evidence.
- **PM / CIO (secondary).** Scans the Command Center for portfolio posture and
  "what changed." Needs material risk at a glance and the freshest signal first.
- **Head of Research / QA (secondary).** Owns coverage health, the CP-5 QA gate,
  and governance — completeness, freshness SLAs, audit trail.

Their shared context: dense, multi-window, numbers-heavy work where a wrong read
has real money behind it. They are specialists; they value precision over
hand-holding and will not tolerate decoration that costs them information.

### Brand Personality

**Three words: precise, defensible, alert.**

The interface should make the analyst feel four things at once — and they are
not in tension, they are layers:

- **Calm institutional authority.** Output looks committee-ready. No drama, no
  noise, nothing that reads as a consumer app. Restraint is the default.
- **Trading-desk alertness.** Live state, freshness, and "what changed" feel
  immediate. Pulse and motion are reserved for things that are genuinely live.
- **Confident clarity.** Dense complexity is made legible. The user feels on top
  of a large amount of information, never buried by it.
- **Trust through transparency.** Every number is one click from its source.
  Evidence → risk mechanic → credit implication is always reachable. The UI
  earns trust by showing its work, never by asserting.

Voice/tone in copy: terse, technical, exact. Label like a desk, not a brochure
(`Net leverage`, `3Y DM +504bps`, `pack HELD`). No marketing language, no
exclamation, no emoji in product chrome.

### Aesthetic Direction

**A refined institutional terminal — a *designed* Bloomberg, not a raw one.**
Keep the dense, dark credit-desk feel, but make every pixel intentional. The
current code already trends here; the job is to hold that bar, not to soften
toward generic SaaS and not to degrade into raw utilitarian clutter.

**Established visual system (inherit, don't reinvent):**

- **Theme: dark, single mode for the workspace.** Surfaces ramp from
  `--caos-bg #0a0a0f` → `--caos-panel #11131d` → `--caos-elevated #1d2030`,
  hairline borders `--caos-border #34384a`, text `--caos-text #e6e6ef`, muted
  `--caos-muted #a1a1b5`, accent blue `--caos-accent #63a1ff`.
- **Semantic color is signal, never decoration:** warning `#f5a524`,
  critical `#ef4444`, success `#22c55e`, idle `#3f3f46`. Color earns its place
  only when it carries meaning.
- **Seniority / tranche ramp is categorical** (1L teal `#2dd4bf`, 2L blue
  `#4f8cff`, unsec amber `#f5a524`, sub purple `#a855f7`, equity slate
  `#64748b`) — distinct hues with no lightness banding, so capital structure
  reads correctly.
- **Typography:** Inter (sans) + JetBrains Mono (mono). All numerics are
  monospace with `tabular-nums` and aligned decimals (`.tabular`). Labels are
  small (9–12px), uppercase, letter-spaced. The 32px uppercase panel header
  (`<Panel>`) is the structural unit of the whole app.
- **Motion:** 160ms ease-out transitions (`.transition-caos`); pulse
  (`.caos-running`) only for live/running state; subtle enter (`.caos-enter`).
  Always honor `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- **Output aesthetic (Report Studio) is a deliberate counterpoint:** a light
  "paper" tear-sheet — ink `#16161e` on cream, monospace mastheads, hairline
  rules, print-ready. Reports look like a document an institution would file,
  not a screenshot of the app.
- **Filed output typography is explicit:** paper titles use 21px/650, section
  titles 14px/650, body copy 13px/400/1.6, mono metadata 8.5px/500, and compact
  table labels 7.8px/600. The full-model appendix is a fit-to-page exception:
  14px title, 7px metadata, 6.5px submetadata, and 5.2–5.9px table text. This
  scale is valid only inside Report Studio, Query/print roots, and research
  paper deliverables; it must not leak into workspace chrome or analytical UI.

**Anti-references — what this must NOT become:**

- Not a friendly consumer SaaS dashboard (oversized type, pastel cards, rounded
  everything, generous empty space, illustrative spot art).
- Not a raw, unstyled terminal dump — density without hierarchy is clutter, not
  craft. Density must always be *organized*.
- No gradients-as-decoration, no glow, no skeuomorphism, no stock iconography.

### Accessibility & Inclusion

**Target: WCAG 2.1 AA, and colorblind-safe.**

- Body and label text meets **4.5:1** contrast against its surface; large/bold
  text meets 3:1. Validate the small muted labels (`--caos-muted` on
  `--caos-panel`) specifically — they sit near the line.
- **Status and tranche meaning is never carried by color alone.** Pair every
  semantic color with a non-color cue: a glyph/shape, a text label, or position.
  (The tranche ramp already avoids lightness banding; keep adding redundant
  encodings.)
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for every animation (already wired — keep it
  that way for anything new).
- Interactive surfaces are keyboard-reachable with a visible focus ring; the
  cross-pane "Evidence Sync" selection should be operable without a mouse.

### Design Principles

1. **Density with hierarchy.** More on screen is good *only* when the eye knows
   where to go. Earn density with clear grouping, the panel idiom, and
   consistent rhythm — never with raw cramming.
2. **Color is signal, not decoration.** Reserve hue for status, seniority, and
   selection. A surface that adds color without adding meaning is a regression.
3. **Show your work.** Every conclusion stays one interaction from its evidence.
   Traceability is a feature of the UI, not a footnote — design for the click
   that proves the number.
4. **Motion only for life.** Animate what is genuinely live or changing (running
   modules, fresh alerts, selection). Decorative motion erodes the calm
   authority the product depends on; always degrade gracefully under
   reduced-motion.
5. **Committee-ready by default.** When in doubt, choose the option that would
   survive scrutiny in front of an investment committee: precise labels,
   defensible numbers, restrained styling. Polish means *intentional*, not
   *ornamented*.
