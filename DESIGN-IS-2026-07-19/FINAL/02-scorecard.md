# Dieter Rams scorecard

1. Good design is innovative — Score: 2/3
   Evidence: `01-evidence.md#usefulness-information-architecture-and-personas` shows a restrained surface × persona composition contract and orthogonal authority states layered onto established institutional-workbench patterns.
   Justification: CAOS materially improves known terminal/workbench patterns but does not prove a pattern absent from five or more peer products.

2. Good design makes a product useful — Score: 3/3
   Evidence: `01-evidence.md#usefulness-information-architecture-and-personas` documents direct analyst, PM, and QA leading tasks, one page primary, and preserved authorized inventory.
   Justification: Every primary persona task is directly supported without decoy actions or permission-changing persona theatre.

3. Good design is aesthetic — Score: 3/3
   Evidence: `01-evidence.md#visual-system-and-hierarchy` and the cited Command, Model, Pipeline, Monitor, and Report captures show one deliberate type, spacing, color, and surface system.
   Justification: The worst representative surface remains visibly systematic; the paper Report is an intentional output mode rather than an orphan style.

4. Good design makes a product understandable — Score: 3/3
   Evidence: `01-evidence.md#usefulness-information-architecture-and-personas` records task-language navigation, analyst stages, direct actions, and capability codes relegated to secondary notation/Advanced disclosure.
   Justification: Primary controls identify their effect in user language; specialist CP codes provide precision without carrying the navigation alone.

5. Good design is unobtrusive — Score: 3/3
   Evidence: `01-evidence.md#visual-system-and-hierarchy` shows content-dominant spreadsheet, worklist, dependency, and paper surfaces with sparse-state framing removed.
   Justification: Chrome supplies authority and navigation but consistently recedes behind the analytical artifact.

6. Good design is honest — Score: 3/3
   Evidence: `01-evidence.md#honesty-and-action-truth` cites exact live/reference isolation, four-axis completion, outcome-derived upload copy, and producer-owned notification labels.
   Justification: Claims, badges, modes, and actions map one-to-one to behavior across the audited truth boundaries.

7. Good design is long-lasting — Score: 3/3
   Evidence: `01-evidence.md#visual-system-and-hierarchy` documents a restrained institutional terminal and filed-paper language with no gradients, glass, decorative illustration, or trend typography.
   Justification: The visual language is operational and durable rather than tied to a fashionable dashboard motif.

8. Good design is thorough down to the last detail — Score: 3/3
   Evidence: `01-evidence.md#accessibility-and-responsive-evidence` and `#technical-quality-and-regression-evidence` cover empty/loading/error/unavailable/completion/focus/reduced-motion/zoom states plus 1,829 passing tests.
   Justification: State, focus, overflow, motion, tablet, zoom, and failure edges are explicitly implemented and measured.

9. Good design is environmentally friendly — Score: 1/3
   Evidence: `01-evidence.md#performance-and-resource-evidence` records motion gating and Report at 261.3KiB, but the worst startup-JS route remains 596.2KiB and Model mounts 3,980 DOM nodes.
   Justification: Resource behavior is measured and motion is responsible, but the worst absolute startup weight crosses the rubric's 500KiB boundary.

10. Good design is as little design as possible — Score: 2/3
   Evidence: `01-evidence.md#visual-system-and-hierarchy` records sparse-frame removal and progressive disclosures, while `#performance-and-resource-evidence` shows residual Model/Portfolio structural weight.
   Justification: Almost every visible element is task-bearing, but the heaviest analytical surfaces still have more initial structure than the minimum necessary.

Total: **26/30**
