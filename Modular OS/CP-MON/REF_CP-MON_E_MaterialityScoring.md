# REF_CP-MON_E — Materiality Scoring
# Version: 1.1 | 2026-06-10 | Evidence discipline and priority hierarchy added (from CP-2B credit interpretation hierarchy)

Score = relevance × mechanism × source authority, adjusted for boosters and penalties. Relevance: legal/LEI highest; sponsor capped. Mechanism: PD 1.00, liquidity 0.95, debt service 0.90, covenant/refinancing 0.85, monitoring 0.30. Boosters: CP-2B trigger, gap fill, cluster, multi-source. Penalties: duplicate, stale, unverified.

## Priority Hierarchy (mechanism calibration, per CP-2B Credit Interpretation Hierarchy)
1. **Highest:** liquidity exhaustion; covenant breach; near-term refinancing failure; debt-service incapacity; maturity wall combined with EBITDA/FCF deterioration; legal/structural deterioration affecting recovery or priming risk.
2. **High:** EBITDA/FCF deterioration impairing deleveraging, market access, or covenant headroom.
3. **Medium:** margin/revenue volatility pressuring but not yet impairing liquidity, refinancing, or debt service.
4. **Lower:** long-dated strategic risks without a clear pathway to cash flow or creditor outcomes.

## Evidence Discipline
- A score is assigned only to sourced signals; unverified social/press signals are capped per domain governance and never reach Critical on their own.
- Missing evidence does not raise a score — absence of information is a gap, not an adverse signal.
- The score rationale must state the risk mechanic (which credit channel and why), not just the channel weight.
- Sponsor-identity signals without documented action are capped (identity is not behavior, per CP-2D standard).
