CP QA Policy v1.0

QA Boundary

CP-5 is the research-integrity QA gate. CP-5B is the evidence-trace
validation gate. Debate modules do not replace QA.

Committee Readiness Gate

An output may be committee-ready only if material claims have evidence trace,
confidence, limitation flags, validation status, and no unresolved critical
contradictions.

QA Status Enums

Two separate status enums are maintained per CP_CANONICAL_STATE_RULES.txt:

qa_status (owned by CP-5):
- Not Reviewed → Passed | Restricted | Blocked

committee_status (determined by CP-5 clearance decision):
- Draft Only → Committee Ready | Restricted | Blocked | Requires More Work |
  Insufficient Information

These are distinct enums. qa_status reflects QA audit outcome. committee_status
reflects committee-use clearance. Both must be set and preserved in all
downstream handoffs.

QA Severity Engine (owned by CP-5):
- Any Critical finding → qa_status = Blocked
- Any Material, no Critical → qa_status = Restricted
- Only Minor or none → qa_status = Passed

QA Prohibitions

QA may not create new analysis, recalculate metrics, reinterpret legal
documents, or invent recommendations. QA may flag, restrict, or block but
must never silently repair malformed schema, unsupported claims, source
conflicts, or missing evidence.
